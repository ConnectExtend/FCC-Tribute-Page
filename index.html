Important moments in Johnny Cash’s life and career

1932
Born on February 26 in Kingsland, AR
1935
Family moved to a 20-acre cotton farm in northeast AR. During his youth, Johnny learned to play the guitar and was influenced by folk songs, hymns, work songs, Irish and gospel music, as well as the realities of life during the Great Depression
1950
Enlisted in the U.S. Air Force and was stationed in Germany as a Morse Code operator
1954
Married his first wife, Vivian Liberto; they had four children and, in 1966, were divorced as a result of Johnny’s addition to prescription drugs and alcohol
Mid-1950s to mid-1960s
Recorded many hit songs, including I Walk the Line, Ring of Fire, The Ballad of Ira Hayes, and Understand Your Man. Made appearances on The Ed Sullivan Show and The Tonight Show and performed more than 200 shows a year
1968
Overcame drug addiction with the help of his singing partner, June Carter; they married in 1968 and their son was born in 1970
Late-1960s to early-1990s
Recorded more hit songs, including Folsum Prison Blues, Jackson, A Boy Named Sue, and Man in Black, and received major honors, including multiple Grammys, and induction into the Country Music Hall of Fame and the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. He also became part of The Highwaymen with Willie Nelson, Waylon Jennings, and Kris Kristofferson
Mid-1990s to late-2002
Became known to a new generation of fans through a partnership with producer Rick Rubin and collaborations with, among others, Tom Petty and U2; three of five American Recordings albums were released and included covers of songs by Depeche Mode, Nine Inch Nails, and Neil Diamond
2003
Passed away on September 12 in Nashville, TN, due to complications of diabetes (four months after June’s passing). The final two American Recordings albums were released posthumously
